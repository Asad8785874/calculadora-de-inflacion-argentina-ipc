How Does Inflation Work in the United States?

The United States has maintained a relatively stable inflation environment, with the Federal Reserve targeting an annual inflation rate of around 2%. This monetary policy framework has helped maintain economic stability and predictable price levels.

Consumer Price Index (CPI) in the US

The Bureau of Labor Statistics (BLS) calculates the CPI monthly, which is the primary inflation indicator in the United States. This index measures the price changes of a representative basket of goods and services consumed by American households.

Factors Influencing US Inflation

US inflation is influenced by factors such as Federal Reserve monetary policy, employment levels, energy prices, housing costs, and global economic conditions. The Fed uses various tools including interest rates to manage inflation and maintain economic stability.
